How to Play “The Bogey” Card Game

1. Game Overview

  • A one-player card game.
  • Goal: get all 52 cards of a standard deck out of your hand and the deck onto the table in as few columns as possible, never exceeding 12 columns.
  • Play alternates: Player turn → Bogey turn → Player turn …
  • You control both your own cards and where the Bogey’s cards are placed.

2. Components & Set-Up

  • 1 standard 52-card deck (no Jokers).
  • Table areas:
    • Talon (face-down draw pile)
    • Discard pile (face-up)
    • Up to 12 columns (stacks of cards on the table)
  • Steps:
    1. Remove Jokers.
    2. Shuffle; this is the talon.
    3. Deal 5 cards to your hand.
    4. Begin with the Player’s turn.

3. Core Mechanics – Columns

  • Each column is a descending, same-suit sequence.
  • Order of ranks: K (high), Q, J, 10, 9 … 2, A (low).
  • Building rules:
    • Any card may start a new column if fewer than 12 columns already exist.
    • On a non-empty column you may play only a card of the same suit and lower rank than the top card.
    • Ranks may be skipped (e.g., K → 8 is legal).
  • Once an Ace tops a column nothing more can be added to that column.

4. Turn Structure

A. Player Turn (always begins with 5 cards in hand)

For each card in your hand (5 in normal mode, 4 in the hard mode) choose one:

  • Play it to a legal column (new or existing).
  • Discard it face-up to the discard pile.
  • Hold it – leave it in your hand for future turns.

After every card has been assigned one of these three fates, the turn ends. Passing: If you hold all cards, the Bogey will immediately take one extra turn for every card you held (see below).

B. Bogey Turn

  1. Draw the top card of the talon.
  2. You choose where to place it:
    • onto any legal column, or
    • start a new column only if fewer than 12 columns exist.
  3. If no legal placement exists (no column accepts it and the 12-column limit blocks a new one) the game is lost.

C. Hand Refill

After the Bogey’s last card of the sequence is placed, draw cards from the talon until your hand is back to 5 cards (or 4 if using the hard mode).

  • If the talon is empty at refill time, first reshuffle the entire discard pile face-down to form a new talon, then draw.

5. Deck Exhaustion & Redeals

  • Whenever the talon is empty and a draw is required (for Bogey or for refill), reshuffle the discard pile to create a new talon.
  • There is no limit to the number of redeals.

6. Game End Conditions

  • Win: All 52 cards are legally placed in columns (any number ≤ 12).
  • Loss:
    • Cannot legally place the Bogey’s drawn card, or
    • Any rule would force a 13th column.

7. Scoring / Win Criterion

Fewer columns = better result.

  • 12 = Novice
  • 11 = Normal
  • 10 = Advanced
  • 9 = Legendary
  • 8 = Epic

8. Variant & Extra Notes

  • Hard mode: start with a hand of 4 cards instead of 5.
  • Discards are face-up so you always know which cards have been removed.
  • Held cards remain in your physical hand and are re-evaluated every future turn.
