Getting Back on the Court: 6-Week Basketball Prep

Week 1: Back to Basics

This first week is all about getting your body moving the way it's supposed to. We're laying down the groundwork before we ask you to jump or sprint.

Daily Tune-Up (10-15 minutes): Start with 3-5 minutes of light cardio, breathing through your nose. This gets your heart pumping without overdoing it. Then, we'll work on getting your hips moving freely with some "90/90" drills and crossover steps. This helps your pelvis get back in the right position for athletic moves. Finish up with gentle, multi-directional steps like cariocas and lateral shuffles – just get comfortable moving in all directions without pushing it.

Finally, we'll wake up your feet and ankles. Think of it like oiling rusty hinges. Simple toe raises and ankle circles will get those small, often-ignored foot muscles working. This might seem minor, but it's a huge step in preventing injuries later.

Key Workouts (Wednesday & Saturday): Twice a week, add in some "locomotive" skips – forward and backward – for a minute each, with a short break. The goal here isn't to jump high, but to feel how your body absorbs force and springs back. Quality over intensity, always.

Week 2: Gentle Pushes

Now that your foundation is set, we'll start adding a little bit of impact, but very carefully. It's about getting stronger without overdoing it.

Daily Tune-Up (10-15 minutes): Keep your Week 1 routine, but introduce "metronome jumps." These are small, rhythmic two-legged jumps that build up your tendons' springiness without putting too much stress on them. Start with just 40 seconds of jumping, followed by a good minute of rest, repeating three times. Focus on landing softly and getting off the ground quickly, not on how high you jump.

Your foot and ankle work gets a little tougher now with resistance bands for ankle circles and some single-leg balancing. These target those crucial stabilizing muscles around your ankles—critical structures that absorb basketball's landing forces and prevent rolling injuries.

Key Workouts (Tuesday & Friday): Twice a week, you'll do "Joint Juice" sessions. These are dynamic stretches and movements that really open up your joints. We'll hit common tight spots like your quads and hip flexors with moves like the front chain stretch with a reach and turn. They feel gentle, but you'll notice a big difference in how easily you move.

Week 3: Basketball Moves

This week, we're making things more specific to basketball. We're taking that general athleticism you've built and translating it into game-ready movements.

Daily Tune-Up (12-18 minutes): Your daily sessions now include "3D diagonal step-throughs," which are perfect for mimicking defensive slides on the court. These multi-plane movements build coordination alongside strength. Your metronome jumps get longer, now up to 55 seconds, making those tendons and muscles even springier.

We're also adding single-leg "eccentric rotations." These are controlled movements that mimic how you plant and cut on the court, strengthening your joints through deceleration—basketball's most injury-prone phase of movement. This "deceleration" is actually where most injuries happen in basketball, so we're building your armor here.

Key Workouts (Monday & Thursday): Twice a week, you'll work on your adductors – those inner thigh muscles crucial for side-to-side movement. "Copenhagen assisted lift-offs" will progressively strengthen them, starting with holds and moving into fuller movements as you get stronger. They're often overlooked, but vital for basketball.

Week 4: Stepping Up the Intensity

Now we're increasing both the force and the speed of your movements. Your body is ready to handle more, and we're pushing it a bit while still keeping an eye on your form.

Daily Tune-Up (15-20 minutes): Your metronome jumps are now a full minute long, really building that elastic strength. We're also adding bear crawl stability work to improve how your core transfers power to your arms and legs—essential for basketball's frequent transitions between running, jumping, and lateral movement.

Single-leg calf raises will strengthen the back of your lower leg, giving you more pop for jumping. And "staggered stance respiration chops" will tie your breathing to rotational movements, much like throwing a pass, while also boosting core stability.

Key Workouts (Wednesday & Saturday): These are your recovery days, but they're active ones. We'll use foam rolling to iron out common tight spots in basketball players: calves, hamstrings, quads, and your upper back. Finish with some "knee pumps" and full-range ankle circles to help clear out any gunk from those hard-working lower leg muscles.

Week 5: Unleashing Power

The fifth week emphasizes power development through increased speed of movement while maintaining technical excellence. This phase translates strength gains into useful basketball athleticism.

Daily Tune-Up (15-20 minutes): We're bringing in A-skips and B-skips, which are sprint drills that teach you proper foot placement and knee drive essential for basketball's acceleration demands. Box jumps make their debut here, but start with low boxes. The goal is perfect landing mechanics, not how high you can jump yet.

"Hip shift mechanics" work corrects common movement asymmetries that could otherwise lead to injury when returning to high-intensity play. This seemingly subtle work addresses basketball's frequent side-to-side demands that can create imbalances.

Key Workouts (Tuesday & Friday): Zone 2 cardio extends to 25 minutes twice weekly. This aerobic development builds the cardiovascular foundation necessary for basketball's repeated sprint demands. This isn't the most glamorous part, but it builds your endurance – that ability to recover quickly between sprints and explosive plays during a game. Think of it as building your gas tank.

Week 6: Game On!

This is it – the final week where we tie everything together. We're integrating all your new strength and power into actual basketball movements, ramping up the intensity to match game demands.

Daily Tune-Up (15-20 minutes): Sessions now include combination drills that link sprint-stop-jump sequences. These movement combinations directly replicate basketball's specific demands—driving to the rim, pulling up for jump shots, and defensive slides leading to contested rebounds.

Lateral crossover movements with resistance (bands or cables) strengthen your body's ability to accelerate and decelerate while changing direction. Reactive drills now require you to respond to external cues rather than following predetermined patterns, developing the neurological readiness basketball demands.

Final Assessment (Sunday): Your final session includes a simulated basketball movement circuit: defensive slides transitioning to sprints, followed by jumps or reaches, and completed with backpedaling. This circuit replicates actual game demands while providing a final assessment of your readiness to return to play.

Throughout these six weeks, listen to your body. This program works because we're gradually building you up and giving you enough time to recover. Follow it, and you'll step onto the court feeling springy, athletic, and much more resistant to injury. You'll be ready to play.

Alternative Approach

General Guidelines

  • Daily Time: 10-20 minutes (adjust sets/reps as needed).
  • Focus: Rotate between Mobility/Strength Days and Recovery/Movement Quality Days.
  • Warm-Up: Integrate breathing drills into exercises (e.g., nasal breathing during hip lifts).
  • Progression: Add 1 set or 30 seconds to exercises weekly if time allows.
  • Equipment: Bodyweight-only; substitutions provided for band/cable exercises.

Exercise Prescriptions

(10-20 minutes/day; pick 4-6 exercises)

1. Nasal Breathing Warm-Up

  • Time: 2-3 minutes
  • How: Fast walk, jog, or stationary bike while breathing through the nose.

2. 90/90 Supine Hip Lift

  • Sets/Reps: 1-2 sets × 5 breaths
  • Time: 2 minutes
  • Focus: Hip mobility and diaphragmatic breathing.

3. Supine Alternating Cross Connect

  • Sets/Reps: 1-2 sets × 5 breaths/side
  • Time: 3 minutes
  • Focus: Core stability and thoracic rotation.

4. Half Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

  • Sets/Reps: 1 set × 8 breaths/side
  • Time: 3 minutes
  • Regression: Seated figure-4 stretch if unable to kneel.

5. Push-Up Plus

  • Sets/Reps: 1-2 sets × 6-8 reps
  • Time: 4 minutes
  • Regression: Incline push-ups (hands on bench).

6. Copenhagen Assisted Lift Off

  • Sets/Reps: 1-2 sets × 20-sec hold/side (Level 1)
  • Time: 4 minutes
  • Focus: Adductor and core strength.

7. Bear Crawl (Stability)

  • Sets/Reps: 1-2 sets × 4 steps forward/backward
  • Time: 3 minutes
  • Focus: Shoulder stability and hip coordination.

8. Reverse Crunch Progressions

  • Sets/Reps: 1-2 sets × 8 reps (Level 1: knees bent)
  • Time: 3 minutes
  • Focus: Lower abdominals.

9. Full Range Ankle Circles

  • Sets/Reps: 1 set × 30 sec clockwise + 30 sec counter-clockwise
  • Time: 2 minutes
  • Focus: Ankle mobility.

10. Cat Camel

  • Sets/Reps: 1 set × 8 reps
  • Time: 2 minutes
  • Focus: Spinal articulation.

Progression Tips

  • Week 1-2: Stick to 1 set/exercise.
  • Week 3-4: Add 1 set to 2-3 exercises.
  • Week 5-6: Increase hold times (e.g., Copenhagen from 20s to 30s).
  • Adjust: Skip exercises causing discomfort; prioritize pain-free movement.

Alternative 2

Daily Template

(10-20 minutes; adjust based on time/energy)
1. Warm-Up (2-3 minutes)

  • Nasal Breathing Jog/Skip: Light jog or skipping while breathing through the nose.

2. Basketball-Specific Prep (8-15 minutes)
(Pick 2-3 drills daily; rotate focus areas)

A. Plyometrics (Low-Impact Start)

  • Pogo Hops
    • Sets/Reps: 2-3 sets × 20-30 sec (focus on springy ankles, minimal knee bend).
    • Goal: Achilles/calf tendon conditioning.
  • Depth Drop to Stick
    • Sets/Reps: 2 sets × 6-8 reps (step off a low curb/step, land softly, hold 2 sec).
    • Goal: Teach proper landing mechanics.

B. Agility & Deceleration

  • Lateral Shuffle to Stick
    • Sets/Reps: 2-3 sets × 5 reps/side (shuffle 5 steps, decelerate abruptly).
    • Goal: Prep knees/hips for defensive slides and cuts.
  • Mini-Band Resisted Sprints
    • Sets/Reps: 3-4 sprints × 10-15m (use a resistance band around waist if available).
    • Goal: Build explosive hip drive.

C. Basketball-Specific Mobility

  • Knee-Dominant Jump Prep
    • Sets/Reps: 2 sets × 8 reps (bodyweight squats with a 3-sec eccentric).
    • Goal: Strengthen quads/tendons for jump absorption.
  • Lunge with Rotation
    • Sets/Reps: 2 sets × 5 reps/side (add a medicine ball or backpack for resistance).
    • Goal: Mimic rebounding/passing rotational forces.

D. Recovery/Movement Quality

  • Banded Ankle CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)
    • Sets/Reps: 1-2 sets × 5 reps/ankle.
    • Goal: Improve ankle mobility for quick cuts.
  • Hip Flexor Pulse Stretch
    • Sets/Reps: 1-2 sets × 10 pulses/side (in half-kneeling).
    • Goal: Counteract crouched defensive positioning.

3. Cooldown (2 minutes)

  • Foam Roll Calves/Quads: 30 sec/muscle.
  • Deep Breathing: 5 diaphragmatic breaths in a seated position.
